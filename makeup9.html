Vintage Makeup Looks With MakeupForever

      

 
Eyeliners made the trend since quite a long time now. But, the trend has clearly evolved over the 

past few decades. Each period is embodied by a special shade and an iconic character…
      

 
 50’S LOOK 


EYES

1 – Apply Artist Shadow #M-530 (light shade) over the entire eyelid.

    2 – Using Artist Liner #M-10, apply a line along the upper and lower lash line from 

the outer corner and glide it towards the inner corner, 

making the line thicker at the outer corner.Blend slightly the line on  the upper

 and lower lash line.

3 – Apply Artist Shadow #S-622 (dark shade) on top of your pencil and on the outer

corner of the eyelid. Blend the shadow into the crease no longer than the outer 2 / 3rd

 of the eye to define the crease.
    

 
4 – Apply Artist Shadow #I-538 (medium shade):

• On the upper eyelid, in between the light and the dark shade. Blend it slightly into the light color.

• Just above the crease. Blend it slightly into the dark shade that is in the crease.

5 – Using Ink Liner, trace a Cat Liner. To obtain it, trace a Classic Liner and extend the line to create

a “comma” at the outer corner of the eye.

Apply the Aqua Smoky Lash Mascara from the root of the lash line to the end of the lashes, stretching 

them slightly outwards.
   

    

 
LIPS

1 – Shape the contour of the lips with theAqua Lip #8C. 

Blend the drawn line towards the inside.

2 – Apply Aqua Rouge #8 on the lips.




 60’S LOOK 


EYES

1 – Apply Artist Shadow #M-510 (light shade) on the entire eyelid and under the arch of the brow,

leaving  out the crease of the eye.

2 – Apply Artist Shadow #M-106 (medium shade) along the upper and lower lash lines, correcting

the eye shape if necessary.

3 – Apply Artist Liner #M-10 (dark shade) in the crease defining a rounded arch shape. Blend

slightly the Artist Liner and make sure not to close the outer corner of the eye.
   

    

 
4 – Apply Artist Shadow #M-106 above the crease of the eye. Blend 

it in the dark shade of Artist 

Liner in the crease and the lightshade #M-510 underneath the eyebrow.


5 – Using Ink Liner, trace a Classic Liner:

• Apply the Eyeliner from the center to the outer corner of the eyelid.

• Continue the line from the inner corner to the center of the eyelid, 

joining the two lines to create a full line


Apply the Aqua Smoky Lash Mascara from the root of the lash line 

to the end of the lashes, stretching 

them slightly outwards.

6 – Using Ink Liner, draw false eyelashes along the lower lash line.
    

 
LIPS


1 – Apply Rouge Artist Intense Mat #2 on the lips.
